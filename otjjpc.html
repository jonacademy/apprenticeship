Junior Production Coordinator: OTJ Guide

The "New Learning" Principle: Off-the-Job (OTJ) training is defined as learning which is undertaken outside of the day-to-day work duties and lead towards the achievement of the apprenticeship. It must be completed during your normal working hours in the UK.

Comprehensive List of OTJ Activities

  • Specialised Software Training: Time spent learning Movie Magic Scheduling, Final Draft, or advanced Excel for production budgeting.
  • Production Paperwork Masterclasses: Learning the legalities of drafting call sheets, risk assessments, and location agreements for the first time.
  • Industry Research: Studying the Ofcom Broadcasting Code, ASA regulations, or specific broadcaster editorial guidelines (e.g., BBC, ITV, Channel 4).
  • Technical Shadowing: Observing a Post-Production Supervisor during a grade or dubbing session to understand the delivery pipeline.
  • Financial Mentoring: Structured 1-2-1 sessions with a Line Producer discussing production finance, insurance claims, or union rates (BECTU/Equity).
  • Sustainable Practice: Learning how to use the 'albert' carbon calculator or implementing a new green production workflow.
  • Legal & Business Affairs: Reading and understanding contributor release forms, music clearances, and underlying rights.
  • Portfolio Development: Writing reflective accounts or organising evidence specifically for your End Point Assessment (EPA).

Learning vs. Doing: The Transition

A crucial part of your apprenticeship is the transition from learning a skill to applying it. Once a task becomes part of your regular, independent routine, it is no longer OTJ.

✅ Counts as OTJ

  • Initial Instruction: Being taught how to draft a risk assessment by a Production Manager.
  • Expert Discussion: A meeting where an expert explains the theory or strategy behind a production decision.
  • Feedback Sessions: Reviewing a piece of work with your mentor where you receive constructive guidance on how to improve.
  • New Scenarios: Learning how to manage an international shoot for the first time if you have previously only done UK shoots.

❌ Does NOT Count

  • Repeated Tasks: Drafting your 15th risk assessment once you already know the process. This is now "Business as Usual."
  • Operational Updates: Standard production catch-ups where the team simply discusses the status of current tasks.
  • General Admin: Responding to emails, booking standard travel, or general office maintenance.
  • Functional Skills: Any time spent on Level 2 English or Maths (this is funded separately).

Example Log Entry

Use the following structure to ensure your learning is captured in a way that satisfies the auditors:

Activity Type: Mentoring & Professional Discussion

Duration: 60 Minutes

Description: Structured 1-2-1 session with the Line Producer regarding the negotiation of freelance crew contracts. We discussed the differences between BECTU guidelines and company-specific deal memos, focusing on overtime triggers and kit rental agreements.

Learning Outcome: I have gained a new understanding of the legal requirements for crew engagement and how to calculate potential budgetary overages based on various shift patterns. This knowledge will allow me to assist more effectively with financial forecasting.

Remember: If you didn't learn something new, you can't log it as OTJ!
